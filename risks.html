The Dangers of Public Computers

Part 2: Additional Risks

Fake login pages mimic legitimate sites to steal credentials, leading to identity theft and financial loss. These sites often use convincing designs and promotions to trick users.

Disabled security features on public computers, such as firewalls and antivirus programs, make them vulnerable to malware and attacks like session hijacking and hardware keylogging.

Clipboard monitoring tools silently capture copied data, including sensitive information like passwords and cryptocurrency addresses, posing another invisible threat.
